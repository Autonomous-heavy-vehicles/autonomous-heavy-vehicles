Overview

Welcome to workshop on Off-roads Autonomous Machines Challenges in Shared Environments

Autonomous off-road heavy vehicles, including trucks, excavators, and loaders, are transforming industries such as construction, mining, forestry, and logistics. They enable safer, more efficient, and reliable operations in unstructured and dynamic environments. However, their deployment faces three major challenges: perception in harsh environments, navigation across unstructured terrains, and human-vehicle collaboration in shared workspaces. Perception systems struggle to detect and classify obstacles under harsh conditions such as fog, dust, and uneven lighting. Navigation and motion planning must address difficulties in planning safe and efficient paths on slippery, muddy, or uneven terrains while considering the size, weight and limited maneuverability of the vehicles. A particularly critical challenge is human-vehicle collaboration in shared workspaces. Safety concerns often force all human activities to stop when autonomous vehicles are in operation, causing significant workflow disruptions and inefficiencies. Ensuring effective collaboration requires frameworks that prioritize safety, build trust, and allow humans and autonomous systems to work efficiently and harmoniously alongside one another.

This workshop will bring together robotics researchers, industry experts, and practitioners to explore these challenges as interconnected components of a unified system. By sharing advances in perception, adaptive navigation algorithms, and human-vehicle interaction frameworks, the workshop aims to foster collaboration, identify practical solutions, and define future directions for deploying autonomous heavy vehicles safely and effectively in real-world environments.
